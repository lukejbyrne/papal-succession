# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Readers, teachers, parish groups, Catholic researchers, and curious non-specialists who want a clearer way to browse the popes than a static table.

## Product Purpose

Papal Succession is a source-backed reference site that turns the official list of Supreme Pontiffs into a readable timeline, searchable directory, and individual pope pages. The first version covers popes only, while leaving room for antipopes, bishops, sees, councils, and succession controversies later.

## Brand Personality

Ancient, calm, exact. The site should feel like a well-kept historical register with modern navigation, not a devotional poster or a generic data dashboard.

## Anti-references

Avoid church stock imagery, triumphalist polemics, generic Catholic gold-on-white decoration, and visualizations that imply complexity where the data is simply one ordered sequence.

## Design Principles

- Make the official source obvious.
- Prefer plain language over insider terms.
- Use the timeline for scale and the directory for lookup.
- Treat early and disputed evidence cautiously.
- Surface succession crises where the line is genuinely complicated.
